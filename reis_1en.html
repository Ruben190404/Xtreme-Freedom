Around America in a Volkswagen van

Do you want to make a real free round trip through America? Then this is something for you! You will make that trip in an old Volkswagen van and you will see and experience everything on your journey, that we can guarantee. We love nature and being outdoors and that's why after such a day of freedom you will sleep under three stars.

Grand Teton National Park

On the first day you will drive and stay in Grand Teton National Park. You can enter the park through three entrances located on main roads. The northern entrance is on US-89 and leads to Yellow Stone National Park. To the south of the park, you enter the park from the town of Jackson via the US-89. The eastern entrance is located on US-287 and enters the park at Moran Junction. It is a beautiful landscape and perfect for hiking and wildlife sports. It is beautifully green and there are many animals like beavers, moose, bears etc. You can also visit places like: Mormon row, Snake River, Colter Bay Village, Jenny Lake, Cascade Canyon. When the evening comes, the sky is beautiful and full of stars. You can stay in a hotel, but we don't think that is a real freedom, so we advise you to stay at one of the campsites. A beautiful place you will enjoy very much!

Zion National Park

On the second day, you will unfortunately leave Grand Teton National Park. But luckily there is another good park waiting for you! Namely Zion National Park, you drive away from the park and head towards Zion National Park. You will see a beautiful gorge with a length of 24 kilometers and a depth of 800 meters. The flora and fauna has been carved out of the gorge by the Virgin River. Zion National Park is a real hiking park and especially suitable for the more practiced hiker. A number of hikes are quite tough and require good preparation and stamina. Take plenty of water with you on your hike and be prepared for changing weather conditions such as cold and strong winds. When the night comes, you can choose to stay at one of the three campsites. Lava Point Primitive Campground is a very primitive campground, you will not have access to water. And there are some other campgrounds just like the first one, a beautiful park!

Kenai Fjords National Park

After leaving Zion National Park, drive to the Gateway to the Kenai Fjords National Park (bring warm clothes!). You will be in the town of Seward, which is about 200 kilometers south of the city of Anchorage. You should take about half a day to cover the stretch and from Seward you can easily take the minibus to the Exit Glacier but the rest of the park is fairly inaccessible by road. So you will have to go on one of the boat tours that are offered in the area, which is a great trip. Once you arrive there, there is plenty to do: kayaking, hiking, puffin and wildlife watching, so pick the activities that appeal to you the most and make the most of it!

Yellowstone National Park

After you have finished with Kenai Fjords National Park you will go to Yellowstone National Park, the oldest national park in the world! The park has a beautiful lake it is the largest mountain lake in America and covers an area of 352 square kilometers. Yellowstone Lake is a good place for recreational anglers so bring your fishing gear. You can hire boats and canoes from Bridge Bay and Grant Village and take a boat trip around the lake. Apart from that, there are plenty of things to see, such as Mammoth Hot Springs, Upper Geyser Basin & Old Faithful, Grand Prismatic Spring, Yellowstone Lake, and the wildlife of course. Yellowstone National Park is known for its rich wildlife. The park is home to grizzly and brown bears, bison's, coyotes, deer's, elks and mountain goats, as well as many bird species including the eagle. In winter, you can follow the tracks of wolves in the snow. At the visitor centers you can inquire about the best times of day to spot wildlife. Caution is advised, as approaching bears is not permitted. It is not possible to spend the night here, so you will have to spend it in the van unless you want to drive straight on.

Glacier National Park

And last but not least, Glacier National Park. Glacier National Park is located in the northwest of Montana. 53 km northeast of Kalispell you drive via State Highway 2 into the park via the West Entrance. State Highway 89 enters the park 51 km northwest of Browning at the Saint Mary Entrance. There are beautiful things to be seen here as well, like: Going-to-the-Sun Road, Logan Pass, Lake McDonald, Avalanche Lake, St. Mary Lake. There are also beautiful geysers to see and the weather is nice. In the north-eastern corner of Glacier National Park is Many Glacier, which is considered the heart of the park. Here you can go boating and horse riding (also with a guide), but above all it is a paradise for hikers. You can choose where to sleep there is a hostel where you can sleep, or the Belton Chalet near Lake McDonald. There are also plenty of campsites within the park boundaries, here you can use the Volkswagen van. At Fish Creek and St. Mary Campground you have the option to book in advance, at the other campgrounds it's first come, first served. We are sure you will enjoy these trips!
