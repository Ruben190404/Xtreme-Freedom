Boat trip across the North Sea

This is a wonderful trip across the North Sea that you could experience, the boat trip will last a midweek and makes a tour of the North Sea.
You will pass beautiful places such as Kingston upon Hull, Aberdeen, the island of Mainland, Stavanger and then back to Den Helder.
In the cities/places we pass by are markets and fun activities that you can do such as, Streetlife Museum of The Deep an aquarium which are near Kingston.
On Mainland 1 of Scotland's 790 islands, there are tours that take you past ancient ruins or you do just a walking tour.
In the Norwegian town of Stavanger we are going to catch our own fish at the Norwegian coast on the last day to cook it afterwards, you would be able to choose from salmon or arctic cod.
After eating the fresh fish we pack our things and leave again by boat to Den Helder.

Start of the journey!

This is the first day of your wonderful journey across the North Sea. The journey starts at 10 am in the beautiful harbor of Den Helder. The first boat trip goes to Kingston upon Hull, which is a boat trip of about 7 hours and is 350km long. During the boat trip you can watch fish, we have a collection book of every living organism from the North Sea that you could keep during your trip, it would of course be nice if that booklet is completely full.
In Kingston there is a market nearby a Museum or the aquarium that you could visit.

Day two!

The day starts early as we head today towards Aberdeen a city in the east of Scotland.
When we arrive there around noon there are quite cool things to do such as, the lake of Visit Lochness which receives over 1 million visitors a year or the Scottish Highlands Castle. Or take your rest and visit 1 of the 45 parks, there are also many souvenir shops where you can what fun could bring.
make sure you go to sleep on time because the ries continues on day 3 to the island of Mainland.

Day three!

Today we go to the beautiful northernmost island of Scotland and part of the island groups Orkney Islands and British Isles.
More than 18,000 inhabitants live on Mainland and about 150,000 tourists come every year.
The tourists come there to visit the Old Scatness or the Scalloway Castle, but not all because of it that is why the tourists come there the island has a beautiful view of the North Sea and the Norwegian Sea.

Day four!

Today the boat trip continues to Stavanger a city in Norway.
The city is known for the Preikestolen which is a mountain area with one of the most beautiful views in the world world and good walking routes. Norway is the most sparsely populated country in Iceland and Kazakhstan Europe. many tourists come there not only for the Preikestolen but also for the glacier and the fjords.

End of the journey!

The last day of the trip will be a quiet day.
The boat departs from Stavanger (Norway) to Den Helder at 10 am so we can get around 22:30 arrive in Den Helder.
Breakfast, lunch and dinner are included during the tour.
After dinner you can possibly pour a glass of champagne to toast your wonderful journey.
